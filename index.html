London Underground Development

The history of the London Underground can be traced back to the 19th century. As an unprecedented commute mode, it was designed to solve the increasing ground traffic congestion caused by rapid urban development and boosting population. Soon, this ambitious attempt achieved great success. After the opening of the first tube line in 1863, 9.5 million passengers were delivered in the first 12 months, then this number increased to 12 million by the end of the second year (Simpson, 2003).

The development of the London Underground over the past 150 years has been astonishing, animation on the left side clearly displays its growth. Nowadays, as one of the densest public transportation networks in the world, the London Underground has expanded to 11 lines covering 402 km and serving over five million passengers per day (TfL,2023).
